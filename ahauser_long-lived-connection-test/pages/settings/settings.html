What's the difference between a notarial service and a notarial act?

The main difference between a notarial service and a notarial act is the scope. Notarial services refer to the comprehensive suite of functions a notary public offers, while notarial acts are the individual tasks performed as part of these services.

e.g. Acknowledgment: A notarial act in which an individual acknowledges to the notary that they have signed a document voluntarily for its intended purpose. Jurat: A certification by a notary that a signer has sworn to or affirmed the truthfulness of the contents of a document and has signed it in the notary's presence. Certification of a Copy: A notarial act in which the notary certifies that a copy of a document is a true and accurate reproduction of the original.
