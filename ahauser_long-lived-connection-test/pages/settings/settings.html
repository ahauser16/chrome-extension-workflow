What's the difference between a notarial service and a notarial act?

The main difference between a notarial service and a notarial act is the scope. Notarial services refer to the comprehensive suite of functions a notary public offers, while notarial acts are the individual tasks performed as part of these services.

Notary Documents

Useful Documents

Acknowledgment Form

This is a statement by the notary public that the signer of a document appeared before them and acknowledged signing for its stated purpose.

Jurat Form

This is a statement from the notary public that a signer has sworn to or affirmed the truthfulness of a document.

Copy Certification Form

This is a form used when a notary certifies that a copy of a document is a true and accurate reproduction of the original.

Oath or Affirmation Form

This is a form used when a notary administers an oath or affirmation.

Signature Witnessing Form

This is a form used when a notary witnesses a signature.

Deposition Form

This is a form used when a notary takes a deposition.

Protest Form

This is a form used when a notary protests a bill of exchange.

Notarial Register

This is a record where the notary keeps track of all notarial acts performed.

Notarial Certificate

This is a form that the notary completes and signs to document the notarial act.

Corporate Acknowledgment Form

This is a form used when a corporate officer acknowledges signing a document on behalf of the corporation.
